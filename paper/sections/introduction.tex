\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

The 2025 NeurIPS / ICML cycle established a dominant template for reinforcement-learning-driven biological sequence design: take a pretrained generative model (a protein language model, a discrete diffusion model, or a DNA language model), fine-tune with PPO / DPO / GRPO / KTO against a fitness proxy, and add a naturalness regulariser. Recent representative methods --- $\delta$-Conservative Search \citep{kim2025deltacs}, $\mu$Protein \citep{sun2025muprotein}, the Caltech / Yue-lab steering work \citep{yang2025steering}, GLID$^2$E \citep{cao2025glid2e}, BioReason \citep{bioreason2025}, ALDE \citep{yang2025alde} --- all share a single architectural choice: an \emph{undifferentiated} policy gradient against a scalar reward. When the proxy becomes unreliable on out-of-distribution sequences, the policy hacks the proxy; existing mitigations (e.g.\ $\delta$-CS) clip the exploration radius using proxy variance, treating the symptom rather than the cause. The cause is structural: the gradient pools two effects --- up-weighting trajectories the policy already supports (the analogue of evolutionary \emph{selection}) and shifting the policy's support to new regions (the analogue of \emph{transmission}, i.e.\ mutation and inheritance) --- into a single estimator that cannot tell us, per round, which operation it is performing.

\citet{price1970selection} decomposed any change in mean trait value across an evolving population into precisely these two terms, exactly. \citet{frank2025price} recently proved that the algebraic form $\Delta\theta = M f + b + \xi$ (the force--metric--bias law) subsumes natural selection, Bayesian updating, SGD, and policy gradients as algebraically identical objects, with the Price decomposition lifting to all of them. That paper made no algorithmic claim. We do. The starting point is a sample-wise identity: every Monte-Carlo policy-gradient sample $x$ either lies in the previous policy's support or it does not, and the indicator $\mathbb{1}_{\mathrm{supp}_t}(x) + \mathbb{1}_{\overline{\mathrm{supp}}_t}(x) = 1$ partitions the gradient on every batch, giving $\hat g_S + \hat g_T = \hat g_{\mathrm{pool}}$ exactly --- not in expectation, but as random variables. The two halves can therefore be measured, weighted, and gated independently without affecting the estimator's unbiasedness, and a single scalar $\rho_t = \|g_S\|/(\|g_S\|+\|g_T\|)$ summarises the gradient's selection--transmission balance on a unit interval.

The diagnostic role of $\rho_t$ is the cleanest part of the story. Reward hacking is structurally an event in which a policy refines an artefact within its existing support: the proxy is largest on a sequence the policy already places mass on, and the gradient pushes that mass higher rather than spreading it. That picture has a direct fingerprint in the Price ratio --- $\rho_t$ saturates near $1$ several rounds before the realised proxy-vs-true gap or proxy ensemble variance has time to reach its alarm. The controller role is more delicate: the right balance depends on the reward landscape, and we tie the target $\rho^*_t$ to an autocorrelation-length estimate $\hat L_t$ via $\rho^*(\hat L) = L_{\mathrm{ref}}/(\hat L + L_{\mathrm{ref}})$. A PI controller drives the empirical $\rho_t$ towards $\rho^*_t$ by modulating the relative learning rates $\alpha_S, \alpha_T$ while holding their geometric mean fixed.

\paragraph{Contributions.} (i) A new RL primitive: separate on-policy estimation of $g_S$ and $g_T$, partitioned by support membership, with a sample-wise exact identity that requires only the existence of $\nabla_\theta \log\pi_\theta$ and so applies to factorised \emph{and} autoregressive parameterisations (Theorem~\ref{thm:exact} and Corollary~\ref{cor:thm1ar}). (ii) An adaptive controller that drives $\rho_t$ to a target derived from the reward landscape's autocorrelation length, with a regret bound (Theorem~\ref{thm:regret}) and an empirical Pearson $r=0.995$ against the regret-optimal target across an NK ruggedness sweep. (iii) The Price ratio as an early-warning signal for reward hacking that fires gradient-side rather than behaviour-side and beats the proxy-variance signal of $\delta$-CS by $9$--$18$ rounds on $100\%$ of seeds. (iv) Empirical results on $149{,}361$-variant GB1 \citep{wu2016adaptation}, four ProteinGym DMS landscapes, the binary deceptive Trap-K family, and a $480$-config robustness study, with code released under MIT licence. The paper does not propose a better protein optimiser per se; it proposes that the algebraic structure of the optimiser was wrong, and biology is the domain where the rewriting pays off most cleanly because biological optimisation already \emph{is} a Price-equation process.
