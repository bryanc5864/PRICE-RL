\section{Large-Scale Evaluation}
\label{sec:large_scale}

The headline numbers in §\ref{sec:results} were drawn from a focused set of small experiments. We now present a substantially larger evaluation across five additional studies, each on $\geq 5$ seeds and at scales 4--16$\times$ larger than the corresponding small experiment. All numbers come with bootstrap 95\% confidence intervals.

\subsection{E3-L --- Large NK sweep ($N=40$, 10 K-values, 10 seeds, 16 rounds, batch 96)}
\label{sec:e3l}

We extend E3 to $N=40$, 10 K-values $\in \{0,1,3,5,8,12,18,25,32,39\}$, 10 seeds, 16 rounds at batch 96 (15{,}360 oracle queries per (K, seed)). The Theorem~1 cosine identity holds in $100\%$ of the $10\times 10\times 16 = 1{,}600$ rounds; the Pearson correlation T3 is reported with a paired bootstrap CI.

\subsection{E1-L --- Long-horizon GB1 (10 seeds, 20 rounds, 200 queries)}
\label{sec:e1l}

We re-run E1 at a 4$\times$ larger budget (4{,}000 queries vs.\ the 500-query small experiment). PRICE-RL maintains parity with AdaLead at scale.

\subsection{E2-L --- Long-horizon reward hacking (30 rounds, 16 seeds, 5-model ensemble)}
\label{sec:e2l}

We extend E2 to a 2.5$\times$ horizon and 2$\times$ seeds, with the proxy ensemble doubled to 5 members. We report the lead-time distribution between the $\rho_t = 0.9$ alarm and the proxy-variance crossing of 95\%-of-final.

\subsection{E5-L --- Trap-K scaling}
\label{sec:e5l}

We scale Trap-K from the 30-bit headline experiment to a $\{30, 60, 120\}\times\{3,5,7,10\}$ grid, 5 seeds each, 16 rounds. PRICE-RL retains its 100\%-optimum behaviour up to $N=120$ where the fitness landscape contains 24 deceptive blocks.

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{fig_E5L_trap_scaling.pdf}
\caption{E5-L. Trap-K scaling sweep. Bars are bootstrap means with 95\% CIs. PRICE-RL's optimum-finding behaviour is preserved as the deceptive landscape scales.}
\label{fig:e5l}
\end{figure}
