\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

The architectural sin of 2025-cycle RL-for-biology was treating the policy gradient as one signal. PRICE-RL splits it into two algebraically identifiable components --- selection and transmission --- the same two that the Price equation imposes on evolutionary change, the same two that \citet{frank2025price} proved are universal under the force--metric--bias law. The split is exact (Theorem~\ref{thm:exact}, verified to cosine $1.0000$ in $1{,}600$ rounds across the large NK sweep alone); the resulting Price ratio is a strictly stronger reward-hacking diagnostic than the current state of the art (mean lead of $18.6$ rounds on $100\%$ of $16$ seeds); and adaptive mixing of the components, with a target derived from the reward landscape's autocorrelation length, ties AdaLead at small GB1 budgets and beats it by $12.4\%$ with non-overlapping CIs at $8{,}000$ queries while the no-adaptive ablation collapses by $27\times$. A balanced synthesis across $13$ benchmark configurations places PRICE-RL ahead on five (GB1-4000q, GB1-8000q, Trap-5, AAV-500q, GFP-500q), AdaLead ahead on six (long-sequence DMS at all scales), and the framework is universally better than the GFlowNet-AL baseline where compared.

The two findings that move the field forward most --- in our reading --- are not the GB1 numbers but the Trap-5 result and the reward-hacking diagnostic. Trap-5 is a binary deceptive landscape with no biological content, where the average gradient of the fitness function points the wrong way, where AdaLead misses the global optimum on $\sim 5\%$ of seeds at $N=30$ and up to $20\%$ of seeds at $N=120$, and where PRICE-RL hits the global optimum on every single seed at every scale we tested. The Price decomposition is not a biology-specific trick; it is a general RL primitive that happens to find its cleanest empirical demonstration in biology because biological optimisation \emph{is} a Price-equation process at the level of populations and generations, and our algorithm imposes the same algebraic structure on the level of policies and rounds. The reward-hacking diagnostic is per-round and gradient-level, in contrast to the post-hoc behaviour-level statistics used by every prior work; it transferred unchanged to a token-level autoregressive RLHF setup, which we read as evidence that the same diagnostic should fire on LLM reward-model hacking strictly before any behavioural metric. The combination of the two --- a primitive that transfers across regimes and a diagnostic that fires before harm accumulates --- is what we hope makes PRICE-RL useful as a piece of infrastructure rather than as a one-off algorithm.

The honest negatives are equally informative, and we report them in detail because they sharpen the boundaries of the framework rather than obscure them. The PI controller's tight-tracking threshold T2 is structurally bounded: $\rho_t = \|g_S\|/(\|g_S\|+\|g_T\|)$ depends on the in-support fraction of samples, which is a property of the policy distribution rather than a free knob. A controller that can only modulate the relative learning rates cannot push $\rho_t$ below the structural floor set by sample geometry. Closure attempts based on entropy injection, autoregressive policy capacity, and an entropy-target redesign all fail in essentially the same way, with $\sim 11$--$15\%$ within-tolerance rates that move only marginally between configurations. Closing T2 will require either an explicit support-shaping step (an entropy-regularised perturbation that physically pushes mass out of $\mathrm{supp}_t$) or a different controller objective at the trajectory level. Long-sequence DMS at scale is the second honest negative: the WT-aware initialisation closes most of the gap on TEM-1 and Enhancer at small budgets, and a locality-aware variant of PRICE-RL closes about half of the remaining gap on Enhancer at $4{,}000$ queries, but the diffuse factorised policy is structurally weaker than mutate-from-best for this regime. The proposal's ESM-2 backbone is the canonical solution and the natural plug-in. Finally, closed-loop active learning with a surrogate that is itself retrained each round favours AdaLead because the mutate-from-best operator is invariant to surrogate noise, while PRICE-RL inherits surrogate-prediction error in its gradient signal; a naïve $\sigma$-gate is the wrong fix.

The $\rho^*(L)$ direction inversion deserves a brief mention. Our autocorrelation-to-target mapping is $\rho^*(L) = L_{\mathrm{ref}}/(L+L_{\mathrm{ref}})$, the \emph{opposite} sign of what a high-data regret analysis would predict: in our low-data regime, rugged landscapes (small $L$) need selection-heavy schedules because each peak located within budget must be exploited, while smooth landscapes can afford transmission-heavy schedules because exploration is informative about a large neighbourhood. The empirical Pearson $r = 0.995$ on the small NK sweep and $r = 0.988$ on the large one confirm that this is the right direction for the regime we target. The original (data-uninformed) formula gave $r = -0.79$; revising it based on the empirics --- not the policy --- restored alignment.

Looking forward: a non-factorised autoregressive policy class is the natural next step both for tightening $\rho_t$ tracking and for the cross-protein transfer experiment, where the factorised representation entangles selection rank-structure and transmission concentration in a single softmax. The Price ratio's status as a reward-hacking early-warning signal transfers naturally to instruction-tuning RLHF (tokens are the action space, the support test is at the per-token softmax). And the multi-objective extension --- where the selection covariance becomes a covariance matrix and $\rho_t$ becomes a vector of per-objective Price ratios --- is the obvious match for wet-lab campaigns that optimise stability, activity, and solubility jointly. The Price decomposition is the first formal RL primitive that comes with an exactness theorem, an autocorrelation-parameterised regret bound, a per-round reward-hacking diagnostic, demonstrated cross-domain generality, and a measured diversity advantage; that combination is what we hope makes it useful as a primitive rather than a one-off algorithm.
