\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

We test three hypotheses corresponding to the three theorems.

\paragraph{H1 (low-data SOTA, partial).} PRICE-RL matches state-of-the-art active-learning baselines on the 149{,}361-variant GB1 four-mutation landscape \citep{wu2016adaptation} under a 5-round / 100-query budget. The adaptive-mixing ablation collapses, isolating the contribution of the controller.

\paragraph{H2 (reward-hacking diagnostic).} On a deliberately weak proxy surrogate, $\rho_t$ saturates earlier than proxy ensemble variance --- the signal used by $\delta$-CS.

\paragraph{H3 (theory-empirics match).} On NK landscapes \citep{kauffman1989nk} swept across $K\in\{0,1,3,5,10,15,19\}$, the empirical mean $\rho_t$ tracks the analytic regret-optimal $\rho^*_t$ as $K$ varies.

\subsection{Datasets}
\label{sec:datasets}

\textbf{GB1 four-mutation} \citep{wu2016adaptation}, accessed via the FLIP repository \citep{dallago2021flip}: 149{,}361 four-position combinatorial variants of the immunoglobulin-binding protein G domain. \textbf{GFP} \citep{sarkisyan2016local}, \textbf{TEM-1 $\beta$-lactamase} \citep{stiffler2015evolvability}, and \textbf{AAV capsid} \citep{bryant2021deep}, accessed via ProteinGym v0.1 \citep{notin2023proteingym}. \textbf{BCL11A enhancer} \citep{kircher2019saturation}, accessed via MaveDB \citep{esposito2019mavedb}. \textbf{NK landscapes}: $N=20$, alphabet $A=4$, $K\in\{0,1,3,5,10,15,19\}$, with deterministic (Blake2b-keyed) per-locus contributions giving exact reproducibility across machines. The analytic autocorrelation length is $L_{\mathrm{NK}}(K) = N/(K+1)$ \citep{stadler1999random}.

\subsection{Baselines}

We compare against \textbf{Random} (uniform), \textbf{AdaLead} \citep{sinai2020adalead} (greedy evolutionary baseline with edit rate $\kappa = 0.05$ and 8 parents), \textbf{PEX} \citep{ren2022proximal} (proximal exploration with edit-distance regularisation, radius~3). Two PRICE-RL ablations: \textbf{PRICE-RL ($\alpha_S=\alpha_T$)} fixes the mixing, isolating the contribution of the controller, and the \textbf{open-loop schedule} sets $\alpha_S \propto \rho^*_t$ without any feedback. We also compare to a \textbf{random-support} ablation that uses a randomly-coined partition with the same in-support fraction.

\subsection{Protocols}

\textbf{E1 (GB1).} 5 rounds, $B = 100$ per round, 5 seeds. Primary metric: top-1\% recall. Secondary: best fitness reached. Statistical comparison: 10{,}000-resample paired bootstrap on the difference of means, plus non-inferiority test within 1 std.

\textbf{E2 (reward hacking).} NK($N=20, K=10$) with a 200-label MLP surrogate as the proxy reward. PRICE-RL runs for 12 rounds against the surrogate; the \emph{true} fitness is logged but not used by the policy. Eight seeds. The proxy variance signal is computed from a 3-model ensemble.

\textbf{E3 (NK sweep).} Seven $K$ values $\times$ five seeds $\times$ 8 rounds at $B=64$. We log per-round $\rho_t$, $\rho^*_t$, $\hat L_t$, the cosine of $g_S+g_T$ against the pooled REINFORCE gradient (Theorem~\ref{thm:exact}), and best fitness.
