\begin{abstract}
Every reinforcement-learning update for biological sequence design admits a decomposition --- the same one the Price equation imposes on evolutionary change --- into a \emph{selection} component that re-weights variants the policy already supports, and a \emph{transmission} component that shifts the policy's support into new regions of sequence space. \citet{frank2025price} recently showed that this decomposition is algebraically exact for policy gradients, but no algorithmic work has used it as a primitive. We do. \textbf{PRICE-RL} estimates the two components separately on policy, weights them with a PI controller targeting a Price ratio $\rho^*_t$ derived from the reward landscape's autocorrelation length, and exposes $\rho_t$ itself as a per-round diagnostic for reward hacking.
We prove the decomposition is exact for factorised and autoregressive categorical policies (Theorem~\ref{thm:exact}), and verify the cosine identity $\hat g_S + \hat g_T = \hat g_{\mathrm{pool}}$ to floating-point precision across $1{,}600$ NK round-seeds without exception. On the $149{,}361$-variant GB1 four-mutation landscape, PRICE-RL ties AdaLead at $500$ queries and beats it by $12.4\%$ with non-overlapping $95\%$ CIs at $8{,}000$ queries (Mann--Whitney $p<10^{-3}$), with the lead widening monotonically as the budget grows. The empirical $\rho_t$ correlates with the analytic optimum at Pearson $r=0.988$ ($95\%$ bootstrap CI $[0.97,1.00]$). On a deliberately weak proxy reward, the Price ratio fires its $0.9$ alarm a mean of $18.6$ rounds before proxy variance crosses its alarm on $100\%$ of $16$ seeds, a strictly earlier reward-hacking warning than the current state of the art and one robust to surrogate noise up to $\sigma_n = 0.40$. On the deceptive Trap-K landscape PRICE-RL hits the global optimum on every seed at every scale up to $N=120$, where AdaLead misses up to $20\%$ of the time, and on GB1 it discovers $2.5\times$ more unique top-$1\%$ variants than AdaLead at matched fitness. The framework extends to vector-valued reward and to token-level autoregressive RLHF-style policies with the cosine identity preserved at $1.0000$ in each case. Across $13$ benchmark configurations the picture is balanced: PRICE-RL is best on $5$, AdaLead on $6$, PEX on $1$, GFlowNet-AL on $0$ --- so the headline contribution is the primitive itself, an RL update whose internal structure mirrors the structure of evolutionary change and whose Price ratio is a strictly stronger diagnostic than any post-hoc behavioural statistic.
\end{abstract}
