\begin{abstract}
Every reinforcement-learning update for biological sequence design admits a decomposition --- the same one that the Price equation imposes on evolutionary change --- into a \emph{selection} component (re-weighting variants the policy already supports) and a \emph{transmission} component (shifting the policy's support to access new regions of sequence space). \citet{frank2025price} showed that this decomposition is algebraically exact for policy gradients, but no algorithmic work to date has used it as a primitive. We do. \textbf{PRICE-RL} estimates the two components separately on policy, weights them with a PI controller targeting a Price ratio $\rho^*_t$ that depends on the reward landscape's autocorrelation length, and exposes $\rho_t$ itself as a diagnostic for reward hacking. Theoretically, we prove the decomposition is exact for factorised categorical policies under entropy regularisation (Theorem~\ref{thm:exact}). Empirically, across fourteen studies spanning $1{,}600$ NK round-seeds, four GB1 budgets up to $8{,}000$ queries, four DMS protein landscapes, a Trap-K cross-domain scaling sweep up to $N=120$, and a $480$-config robustness study, the Theorem~1 cosine identity holds to floating-point precision in \emph{every single round}. On GB1 the algorithm ties the AdaLead state of the art at $500$ queries and \emph{beats it by $12.4\%$ with non-overlapping $95\%$ CIs} at $8{,}000$ queries (Mann--Whitney $p<10^{-3}$), with the lead widening monotonically with horizon. On NK, the empirical $\rho_t$ correlates with the analytic optimum at Pearson $r=0.988$ ($95\%$ bootstrap CI $[0.97,1.00]$). On a deliberately weak proxy reward, the Price ratio fires its $0.9$ alarm a mean of $18.6$ rounds before proxy variance crosses its alarm on $100\%$ of $16$ seeds --- a strictly earlier early-warning signal for reward hacking than the current state of the art. On the binary deceptive Trap-K landscape (no biological structure), PRICE-RL hits the global optimum on every seed at every scale we tested, while AdaLead misses up to $20\%$ of the time at $N=120$. PRICE-RL discovers $2.5\times$ more unique top-$1\%$ variants than AdaLead at matched fitness on GB1, a biologically critical diversity advantage. The diagnostic is robust to surrogate corruption up to $\sigma_n = 0.40$. We extend the framework to multi-objective reward and to token-level autoregressive RLHF-style policies (Theorem~1 cosine identity preserved at $1.0000$ across both extensions). Across $13$ benchmark configurations PRICE-RL is best on $5$, AdaLead on $6$, PEX on $1$, GFlowNet-AL on $0$.
\end{abstract}
